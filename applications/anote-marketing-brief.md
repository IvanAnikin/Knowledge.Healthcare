> Last updated: 2026-04-24. Based on direct inspection of `ANOTE_mobile` and `ANOTE-web`, plus previously used doctor outreach materials shared during this session.

It focuses on:

- who it is for,

## One-Sentence Description

ANOTE is an AI-powered medical documentation assistant that helps doctors turn spoken patient visits into structured medical reports in seconds, reducing administrative burden and giving more time back to patient care.

## Short Product Description

ANOTE is designed for doctors who spend too much time typing and rewriting clinical documentation after or during patient visits. The product records speech, transcribes the conversation, and generates a structured medical report tailored to the type of examination.

The current ANOTE product presence has two main parts:

- `ANOTE_mobile` — the core mobile application used for real medical dictation workflows.
- `ANOTE-web` — the public marketing website and browser demo used to explain and showcase the product.

## Core Problem ANOTE Solves

ANOTE addresses a clear and painful problem in healthcare: doctors lose a large part of their working time to documentation and repetitive administrative writing.

This creates several downstream problems:

- less time and attention for the patient,
- slower clinical workflow,
- more after-hours documentation,
- greater mental fatigue and burnout risk,
- and more chances for mistakes during manual rewriting.

This problem is already reflected in the team's existing outreach and presentation materials and is strongly aligned with the actual product architecture.

## ANOTE Solution

ANOTE helps doctors document visits faster by turning voice input into structured reports.

In practical terms, the workflow is:

1. The doctor records the visit or dictation.
2. ANOTE transcribes the speech.
3. ANOTE generates a structured medical report in the expected format.
4. The doctor reviews, edits if needed, and copies or sends the result onward.

The product is positioned as documentation support, not as a replacement for clinical judgment.

## Main Value Proposition

ANOTE helps doctors spend less time typing and more time practicing medicine.

## Strongest Selling Points

### 1. Direct time savings

ANOTE is built to reduce the time spent writing anamnesis and visit summaries manually.

This is the clearest commercial benefit and should stay central in marketing.

### 2. More focus on the patient

By reducing typing and post-visit admin work, ANOTE supports a more natural doctor-patient interaction and lets the doctor stay more present during the visit.

### 3. Structured output, not just transcription

ANOTE does not stop at speech-to-text. It turns spoken content into a structured medical report, which is a much stronger value proposition than generic dictation tools.

### 4. Designed for Czech medical documentation

The product is specifically oriented around Czech-language medical use cases and Czech clinical report structure. This is an important differentiator against generic global transcription products.

### 5. Flexible privacy model

ANOTE supports both:

- local/on-device transcription, where audio stays on the device,
- and cloud transcription, where higher speed or accuracy can be prioritized.

This gives a strong privacy and workflow positioning angle.

### 6. Multiple exam/report types

ANOTE is not limited to one generic note type. The inspected applications support multiple visit and examination types, including:

- outpatient or initial examination,
- follow-up visit,
- gastroscopy,
- colonoscopy,
- ultrasound,
- and automatic type detection.

This makes the product feel more specialized and workflow-aware.

### 7. Mobile-first usage in real clinical settings

The mobile application is a practical advantage for doctors who move between rooms, work in outpatient settings, or document care away from a desk.

### 8. Easy output handoff

Generated text can be reviewed, copied, and in some flows sent by email, making ANOTE easier to fit into existing documentation routines without requiring a full system replacement.

### 9. Browser demo lowers adoption friction

The web product includes a live demo, which is valuable for marketing because prospects can quickly understand the workflow before committing to a meeting or pilot.

### 10. Built with direct doctor feedback

The mobile repository contains repeated test-feedback cycles with a real doctor. This supports messaging around practical development, iteration, and product shaping with clinicians rather than building in isolation.

## What ANOTE Is

ANOTE is best described as:

- an AI medical documentation assistant,
- a voice-to-report workflow tool,
- a tool for automatic anamnesis and visit-note creation,
- a productivity product for doctors,
- and a privacy-aware documentation aid for European healthcare contexts.

## What ANOTE Is Not

For accurate positioning, ANOTE should not be described as:

- a replacement for the doctor,
- an autonomous diagnostic system,
- a full hospital information system,
- a patient record system,
- or a certified medical device unless that status is formally established later.

## Security and Privacy Positioning

Security and privacy are among the strongest marketing themes for ANOTE, but the wording should stay precise.

### Strong messaging angles supported by the current product

- local transcription mode is available,
- in local mode, audio can stay on the device,
- text processing is positioned around EU infrastructure,
- the website and product materials emphasize EU data residency,
- and privacy-conscious architecture is a real part of the product story, not just marketing language.

### Safe phrasing to use

- "privacy-first design"
- "on-device transcription mode"
- "audio can stay on the device in local mode"
- "EU-focused infrastructure"
- "built with GDPR-conscious architecture"

### Claims that should be used carefully

- avoid claiming blanket legal compliance beyond what has been reviewed,
- avoid implying certification or regulatory approval,
- avoid absolute statements that no data ever leaves the device in every mode,
- and avoid promising hospital-system integrations that are not yet productized.

## Differentiation Angles

These are the most useful differentiation themes for campaigns and sales materials.

### Specialized instead of generic

ANOTE is more than a generic voice transcription app. It is built around medical documentation and structured report creation.

### Czech-first medical use case

The product is adapted to Czech medical terminology and Czech report structure, which is commercially important in the local market.

### Local plus cloud flexibility

Many tools force one processing model. ANOTE can be positioned around giving doctors or clinics a choice between local privacy-first workflows and cloud-assisted speed/accuracy workflows.

### Workflow compatibility

ANOTE can be described as an add-on or companion workflow rather than a forced replacement of the clinic's existing software.

### Real usability, not just concept

ANOTE already has:

- a working mobile application,
- a deployed backend,
- a live web demo,
- multi-type report generation,
- and documented doctor feedback loops.

That is stronger than presenting it as an idea-stage concept.
