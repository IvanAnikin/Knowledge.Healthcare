## Problem

ANOTE_mobile generates structured Czech medical reports (lékařská zpráva) via its FastAPI backend and Azure OpenAI LLM. Real-world usage by Dr. Jan Brož has surfaced five categories of quality defects in the generated reports. One is a confirmed **clinical safety issue** (allergy hallucination). All five are addressable through prompt engineering without architectural changes.

**Report quality summary from reviewed session:**

|--------|---------|------------|----------------|
| Report 1 | Diabetic, insulin pump | Kontrolní (check-up) | POOR — ~60% irrelevant content, date bug ("13. pondělí") |

The five issues are documented below with verbatim doctor feedback, real report excerpts, and analysis.

### Issue 1 — Irrelevant content included in report sections

**Severity:** Critical

Czech medical reports must contain only clinically relevant information. The LLM currently includes large amounts of off-topic social conversation in medical sections, particularly NO (Nynější onemocnění — Current illness/complaints).

**Doctor's words (Czech):**
> *"Systém nepozná, kdo, co v konverzaci řekl. S těmi pacienty, co znáte dlouho, se bavíte o spoustě věcí, které nijak nesouvisí s jejich nemocemi, třeba je to 90% hovoru."*

Translation: "The system doesn't recognise who said what. With patients you've known for a long time, you talk about many things completely unrelated to their illnesses — that might be 90% of the conversation."

**Real report excerpt (Report 1 — diabetic patient, insulin pump, Kontrolní visit):**

The following appeared in the NO section. None of it is clinically relevant:

> - Popisuje zkušenosti s aktualizací softwaru inzulinové pumpy Medtronic 780, upozorňuje na nutnost dodržet třihodinový proces aktualizace a nepřerušovat spojení telefonu s pumpou.
> - Diskutuje o problémem s technologií, programováním a přetížením AI nástrojů, potýká se s neustálými výpadky služby GPT, která slouží i pro programování.
> - Zmiňuje zkušenost s investicemi, které bývají riskantní, a vlastní zkušenost s finanční ztrátou.
> - Popisuje pracovní zatížení, globální situaci v IT a ekonomické problémy podniků v kontextu energetické krize a války.
> - Diskutuje o výzvách s implementací nových technologií v ČR a globální závislost na velkých hráčích na trhu.

**What NO should contain:** Patient's current medical complaints, symptoms, duration, and relevant medical events only. Technical instructions, opinions on AI tools, financial anecdotes, geopolitical commentary, and social chat are never clinically relevant and must be excluded.

**Additional sections also affected:**
- SA (Sociální anamnéza) in Report 1 includes professional observations about German/Italian business partners, the glass manufacturing industry, and generational work ethic comparisons — none of which was provided in response to a social history question.

**Root cause (prompt):** The LLM prompt does not instruct the model to filter for clinical relevance. It likely instructs "summarise what was discussed" without specifying that only medically relevant content should appear in each section.

### Issue 2 — Physical examination findings placed in NO instead of Objektivní nález

**Severity:** High

Objective, measurable examination findings are appearing in NO (subjective complaints) rather than in Objektivní nález (Objective findings).

**Doctor's words (Czech):**
> *"Patří jen do fyzikálního vyšetření."* (This belongs only in the physical examination section.)

**Real report excerpt (Report 4 — Jiřina Nováková, hypertension + neck swelling):**

In the NO section, the generated report included:
> "Otok krku s hmatnou rezistencí vpravo o velikosti asi 3 cm, elastickou konzistence."

This is a measurable, objective physical finding (a palpable 3 cm elastic mass) — what the doctor found on examination, not what the patient is complaining of.

**Czech medical documentation norm:**
- **NO** = what the patient reports (subjective): complaints, symptoms, onset, duration.
- **Objektivní nález** = what the doctor finds on examination (objective): palpation findings, auscultation, measurable parameters, dimensions of masses.

**Root cause (prompt):** The prompt does not define the boundary between subjective and objective content, nor instruct that measured/observed findings belong in Objektivní nález.

### Issue 3 — Hallucinated / fabricated clinical content (CLINICAL SAFETY ISSUE)

**Severity:** Critical (patient safety)

The model invents clinical content that is not supported by the transcript. This is a generalised correctness principle, not a single brand-name bug:

- The model must never include clinical content (diagnoses, allergies, conditions, dosages) that is not directly supported by what was actually said in the visit.
- If the transcript is ambiguous, garbled, or contains words the model cannot confidently interpret in a clinical sense, the model must **omit the content or flag it as unclear**, never fabricate a plausible-sounding clinical interpretation.
- An incorrect entry is worse than a missing entry. A blank or "neuvedeno" line is safe; a fabricated diagnosis is a patient safety risk.

**Concrete real-world example (Report 4 — Jiřina Nováková):**

The doctor said the patient is allergic to "JAR" (a Czech brand of dish soap). The pipeline produced an output containing "Sezónní alergie na jara, reaguje podrážděním kůže" — a fabricated seasonal pollen allergy.

**Doctor's words (Czech):**
> *"Tady jsem řekl, že na saponát JAR, udělala z toho alergii pyl."*

**Likely chain (from investigation):** Whisper transcribed "JAR" phonetically as "jaro" / "jara" (Czech for "spring"). The LLM then made a clinical inference — "spring → seasonal pollen allergy" — and wrote it as fact.

**The wrong fix would be** to add a special rule about JAR or about pollen. **The right fix** is to instruct the model that:

- Brand names, product names, and substance names mentioned in allergy / medication / family history contexts must be preserved exactly as transcribed, with no inference about what they "really mean".
- When the transcript contains a word that is ambiguous in clinical context (e.g., "jaro", "ibuprofen", an unfamiliar abbreviation), the model must not invent a clinical category. It should write the content verbatim, mark it as unclear (e.g., "k upřesnění"), or omit it.
- Generally: anything that is not clearly supported by the transcript and not clinically meaningful must not appear in the report. Better to leave a section sparse than to fill it with plausible-sounding fabrication.

**Why this is a safety issue:** Allergy and medication entries directly influence prescribing and clinical decisions downstream. A fabricated pollen allergy could lead another clinician to act on false information. The principle applies to all sections — fabricated comorbidities, fabricated dosages, and fabricated symptoms are all patient safety risks.

**Scope note (from investigation):** Eliminating this class of error fully requires Whisper-level work (e.g., medical hotwords for common Czech brand names). The prompt fix can attenuate the issue by forbidding clinical inference on ambiguous tokens; it cannot recover information Whisper already destroyed. This boundary should be clearly communicated when the prompt fix ships.

### Issue 4 — Boilerplate cooperation notes violating Czech documentation norms

**Severity:** Medium

The app adds "Spolupráce dobrá." (Cooperation good) to the Adherence section for every patient, regardless of whether cooperation was discussed.

**Doctor's words (Czech):**
> *"Obvykle se vyjadřujeme ke spolupráci jen pokud nespolupracuje."*

Translation: "We usually only comment on cooperation if the patient is NOT cooperating."

**Real report excerpt (Report 4 — Jiřina Nováková):**
> "Spolupráce dobrá."

This was added automatically. The doctor did not comment on cooperation during this visit.

**Czech medical documentation norm:** Adherence notes are written only when noteworthy — a non-compliant patient, refusal of a procedure, or specific patient education. A blanket "Spolupráce dobrá." signals auto-generation and reduces report credibility.

**Root cause (prompt):** The prompt likely instructs the model to fill the Adherence section with a default value if nothing specific was said. It should instead instruct that this section is omitted unless explicit non-compliance, patient education, or significant adherence-related events are present in the transcript.

### Issue 5 — Medication details paraphrased incorrectly; dosage information lost

**Severity:** High

Specific dosage information spoken by the doctor is replaced with vague paraphrase, and qualifiers not present in the transcript are added.

**Doctor's words (Czech):**
> *"Řečeno 1 tbl."* (Said: 1 tablet.)

**What was spoken:** The doctor specified "1 tbl." (1 tablet) as the dosage for Lusopres.

**What the report generated (Report 4 — Jiřina Nováková):**
> "Pacientka užívá předepsanou medikaci (lusopres) pravidelně."

**Problems:**
1. "1 tbl." (dosage) was dropped entirely.
2. "pravidelně" (regularly) was added — never said; the model inferred it.
3. "předepsanou medikaci" is generic filler that adds no information.

**Correct entry:**
> "Lusopres 1 tbl. ráno."

**Czech medical documentation norm:** FA entries must be precise: drug name + dose + frequency + route, exactly as stated. If dosage was not mentioned, it is absent — not replaced with a vague synonym.

**Root cause (prompt):** The LLM is paraphrasing rather than extracting exact clinical data. The prompt must instruct that medication entries preserve the exact spoken form: drug name as stated, followed only by dosage and frequency as spoken, with no added qualifiers.

## Desired Outcome

After implementation, a generated Czech medical report must satisfy all of the following:

1. **NO contains only medically relevant complaints and symptoms.** Social conversation, technical discussions, personal opinions, financial anecdotes, geopolitical commentary, and any content clearly unrelated to the patient's health is absent from all report sections.

2. **Objective examination findings appear in Objektivní nález**, not in NO. Anything the doctor observed or measured (palpation, auscultation, dimensions, vital signs) goes in Objektivní nález.

3. **No fabricated clinical content.** The model never includes diagnoses, allergies, conditions, or dosages that are not directly supported by the transcript. Brand and substance names are preserved verbatim. When a word is ambiguous in clinical context, the model omits it or marks it as unclear ("k upřesnění") rather than inferring a clinical category. **An omitted entry is always preferable to a fabricated one.**

4. **Adherence/cooperation section is omitted unless the transcript contains explicit non-compliance**, patient education events, or a specific adherence-related discussion. No boilerplate "Spolupráce dobrá." unless the doctor stated this explicitly.

5. **Medication entries preserve exact dosage and frequency as spoken.** No added qualifiers (e.g., "pravidelně") unless explicitly stated. If dosage was not mentioned, the entry notes only the drug name.
